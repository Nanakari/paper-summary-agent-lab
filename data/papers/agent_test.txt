Recent advances in retrieval-augmented generation have improved the factuality of large language models by grounding answers in external documents. However, conventional RAG systems often retrieve irrelevant passages and lack mechanisms to verify whether the generated answer is fully supported by evidence. This paper proposes an agentic RAG framework that decomposes question answering into query planning, iterative retrieval, evidence verification, and answer generation. Experiments on open-domain question answering benchmarks show that the framework improves answer faithfulness and citation accuracy.
